Project Goal

Exploring Quantum & Hybrid Approaches

Investigate whether quantum-inspired methods can offer advantages for anomaly detection in high-dimensional cybersecurity data, compared to strong classical baselines.

The focus is on learning, benchmarking, and understanding scalability and hybrid implications, not on claiming quantum supremacy.

Tech Stack

Stack & Frameworks

Python Scikit-learn Isolation Forest Autoencoders (PyTorch) Qiskit PennyLane Hybrid Quantum-Classical

Approach

Classical vs. Quantum Baselines

  • Classical Baselines: Isolation Forest, One-Class SVM, and deep autoencoders on network/host telemetry.
  • Quantum Circuits: Simple variational circuits for anomaly scoring on reduced feature spaces.
  • Hybrid: Use classical models for feature extraction, quantum layers for final anomaly scoring.
  • Scalability: Analyze how feature dimension and qubit counts affect feasibility.
Use Cases

Cybersecurity Context

  • High-dimensional authentication logs and access patterns.
  • Network flow features with many correlated dimensions.
  • Potential implications for cryptography or optimization tasks in cyber defense.
Reflection

What I Learned

Quantum methods are still early for production SOC use, but exploring them clarifies where they might eventually fit: optimization, feature embedding, or specialized anomaly detection. The main value today is building intuition and hybrid patterns that can evolve as hardware matures.
